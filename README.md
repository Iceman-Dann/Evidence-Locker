Most of it is never recovered — not because people don't have a case, but because they don't know how to build one.

## Features

### Five AI-Generated Documents (Simultaneously)

| # | Document | What It Contains |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Violations Report** | Every applicable federal, state, and local statute. Exact code citations. Plain-English explanation of each violation. Remedies and penalties available. |
| 2 | **Evidence Checklist** | 8–10 specific, actionable items to collect. Not vague advice — specific instructions. 3 items flagged `[CRITICAL]`. Interactive checkboxes that save progress to localStorage. |
| 3 | **Demand Letter** | Complete, professional letter. Today's date. Your facts in numbered paragraphs. Full statute citations. Exact dollar amount. 14-day deadline. Ready to print and mail. |
| 4 | **Filing Roadmap** | Jurisdiction-specific step-by-step guide. NLRB, EEOC, state labor board, small claims — whichever applies. Filing fees, URLs, deadlines, what to say, what not to say. |
| 5 | **Case Strategy** | Probability of success as a percentage. Estimated settlement range in dollars. Recommended approach. Your leverage points. The other party's likely defenses and how to counter them. |

### Everything Else

- **Real-time streaming** — All 5 Gemini API calls fire simultaneously. Text streams into cards character-by-character as it generates. No waiting.
- **Animated severity ring** — SVG circle fills to your case strength (0–100) with easeOutCubic animation. Green = strong, amber = moderate, red = risky.
- **Interactive evidence checklist** — Click to check off items as you gather them. Progress bar shows "4 of 10 items collected." State persists across sessions via localStorage.
- **Auto-generated case timeline** — A 6th API call fires after the first 5 complete, building a scrollable horizontal milestone timeline from Day 1 to resolution.
- **PDF export** — Full formatted case package via jsPDF. Dark cover page, per-section headers, page numbers, case ID in every footer.
- **Share link** — One-click URL generation with case summary encoded as base64 parameter.
- **Keyboard shortcuts** — `D` download PDF, `C` copy all, `S` share, `N` new case, `?` help overlay.
- **Custom cursor** — 8px red dot with 80ms lerp lag. Expands to ring on interactive elements. Makes the product feel considered.

### Why This Architecture

Most AI tools have a backend. EvidenceLocker calls Gemini directly from the browser because:

- **Zero infrastructure cost** — Deploy to GitHub Pages, Vercel, Netlify. Scales to a million users for free.
- **Zero latency overhead** — API calls go straight from user's browser to Google. No round-trip through your server.
- **Zero data liability** — Case data never touches any server you control. It lives exclusively in the user's `localStorage`. You can't subpoena what you don't have.
- **Zero setup friction** — No `npm install`. No `.env` file. No Docker. Open the HTML file.

## Social Impact Statement

The American legal system was built to be navigated by people with legal training and financial resources. Everyone else — which is most people — starts every dispute at a structural disadvantage.

**EvidenceLocker levels that playing field.**

It doesn't give people lawyers. It gives people the same *starting position* that money already buys: a clear understanding of what laws apply, what evidence matters, and what to file first. The kind of knowledge that, right now, costs $300 an hour or requires knowing the right people.

The downstream effects compound:

- When **renters** win deposit cases, landlords stop treating deposits as free money
- When **workers** file FLSA complaints with real documentation, employers calculate that paying overtime is cheaper than the penalty
- When **contractors** receive attorney-grade demand letters, they finish jobs
- When **consumers** know how to escalate, companies process refunds

The individual outcome matters. The **behavioral shift** matters more. EvidenceLocker works not just on individual cases but on the systems that produce the harm in the first place — by making the cost of wrongdoing unpredictable for whoever does it.

**$50 billion stolen annually. Most of it never recovered. Not because the cases aren't there. Because the people don't know they have one.**

## Design Philosophy

Every visual decision in EvidenceLocker was made to communicate one thing: *this is serious.*

Legal tools are typically designed to feel safe, soft, trustworthy — blues and whites, rounded corners, friendly illustrations. That aesthetic is lying to you. It's designed to make a difficult, adversarial process feel comfortable.

EvidenceLocker was designed to feel like what it actually is: **a weapon.**

- **#07070A background** — Not quite black. Darker. More deliberate.
- **One accent color** — A single red, `#C41E1E`, used exactly once per section. The constraint is the design.
- **DM Serif Display** — A typeface with edge. Makes legal language feel like it has weight.
- **JetBrains Mono** — Every label, code, and status in monospace. This is a machine that processes evidence.
- **No rounded corners over 4px** — Soft corners communicate forgiveness. This product doesn't forgive.
- **Custom cursor** — The 8px red dot with lerp lag is the detail that makes judges lean forward. It says someone obsessed over this.

The design goal: a person who has been wronged opens this page and immediately feels less alone, more powerful, and ready to fight.
